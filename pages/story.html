Story time

Or How I Learned To Stop Worrying And Love Socializing Online

Once upon a time, I moved, and had to go to a new school where I didn't know anyone. This was middle school, arguably the worst of the kinds of schools, so I had no friends for a while. What's a girl to do? Seek friendship elsewhere.

Neopets.com is a website where you can create an account, have virtual pets called "neopets," play games, dress up your neopet, and talk to others on the site.

In the early 2000's, it's where a lot of creative kids ended up. It provided a platform for sharing what you can do, like drawing your neopet or making them a webpage (called a "petpage").

At school, nobody cared that I existed. On Neopets.com, I was popular, because I was halfway decent at drawing my pets and entering the art contest.

Ye olde HTML site

In addition to drawing contests, there was a "site spotlight" contest for making an entire webpage dedicated to your pet. It sounded cool, and my older brother was in college studying computer science at the time, so I decided to give it a shot.

Figuring out where to learn HTML at that time was difficult. A site that many users pointed to as a helpful reference was funkychickens.com, which according to the "about" section, was made by a 14-year-old.

It had everything! How to make your colors obnoxious, how to add autoplaying background music that can't be paused, how to make the text blink in and out, and of course, how to make a marquee. It was perfect.

So most of my HTML knowledge became making and styling HTML tables.

My HTML prowess was matched only by my eye for design and color

I decided I would create an amazing, award-winning petpage for my Cybunny named trippinhun. I was 13 at the time, had tried coffee once, and had decided that it was disgusting and that tea was superior in every way, so I would make a petpage all about tea and how awesome it is.

I made some kind of obnoxious, brightly-colored abomination, complete with a bunch of painstakingly illustrated elements, and the four horsemen of the webpage apocalypse: Comic Sans font, everything centered, hidden and unpausable background music, and HTML that nearly made me cry (in the bad way) when I looked back on it 20 years later.

Highlights (or lowlights, really) include HTML comprised of a dozen or so nested "center" tags, and one place where for some reason I wrote "float: center" in the CSS.

And somehow, it worked!

Really, though, it was pretty impressive for the time! I submitted my beautiful abomination for the site spotlight, and actually won!

While I'm sure an 8-year-old today can easily make something way better, I'm proud of what I managed to accomplish all those years ago. This page won the Neopets site spotlight on 08/15/2005, and can still be viewed today.

While I ended up pursuing other interests in college, and after college, eventually it all came back to Neopets somehow. My best friend, who I met through Neopets, recommended me to her boss for an open front end web developer position. And that's what I do for a living now.

If it wasn't for this ridiculous website, and the equally ridiculous petpage I made that won me the site spotlight, I probably wouldn't be a web developer today.
